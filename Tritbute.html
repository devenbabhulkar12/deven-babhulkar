"Missile Man of India" and People's President

Biography

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. A renowned aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India (2002â€“2007), he played a pivotal role in India's civilian space program and military missile development, earning him the title "Missile Man of India." He was also a beloved teacher, author, and visionary.

Achievements

  • Key figure in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.
  • Developed the Agni and Prithvi missiles.
  • Awarded Bharat Ratna, Padma Bhushan, and Padma Vibhushan.
  • Served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007.
  • Author of inspirational books like "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds."

Legacy

Dr. Kalam's legacy lives on through his teachings, writings, and the millions he inspired with his humility, dedication, and vision for a better India. His belief in youth and education continues to motivate generations.
